Monetary Damages **Actual Damages** - Costs of changing locks - Temporary relocation expenses - Lost or damaged personal property - Emotional distress (in some jurisdictions) **Statutory Damages by State:** **California** (Civil Code § 1954) - $100 per violation minimum - Up to $1,000 for willful violations - Attorney's fees available **New York** (Real Property Law § 235-f) - $50-$300 per violation - Treble damages for willful violations - Court costs and attorney's fees **Illinois** (765 ILCS 750/15) - $200 per violation - Additional actual damages - Attorney's fees **Florida** (Fla. Stat. § 83.53) - Actual damages - $100 statutory minimum - Attorney's fees and costs ### 2. Injunctive Relief - Restraining orders against further unauthorized entry - Specific performance of lease obligations - Mandatory injunctions requiring proper procedures ### 3. Lease Termination Rights **Immediate Termination:** - Material breach of lease terms - Violation of statutory entry requirements - Pattern of harassment **Notice and Cure Periods:** - Varies by jurisdiction (3-30 days typical) - Some violations may be non-curable - Emergency situations may allow immediate termination ### 4. Constructive Eviction Claims **Elements Required:** 1. Substantial interference with use/enjoyment 2. Landlord's intentional or negligent conduct 3. Tenant's vacation of premises within reasonable time 4. Notice to landlord of conditions (in most jurisdictions) **Unauthorized Entry Context:** - Must be part of pattern of harassment - Single entry rarely sufficient - Combined with other lease violations strengthens claim **Remedies Available:** - Termination of lease obligations - Return of prepaid rent/deposits - Relocation expenses - Consequential damages ### 5. Self-Help Remedies **Lock Changes** - Generally permitted with notice to landlord - Landlord copy may be required - Costs typically tenant's responsibility **Rent Withholding** - Limited availability - Usually requires material breach - Statutory procedures must be followed ## VI.
